# TEDS-self-harm-Cholesky-decomposition

This work is published alongside other analyses and results at https://doi.org/10.1111/jcpp.14096

Using data from a longitudinal population-based sample of twins – the Twins Early Development study (TEDS), we investigated the relative genetic and environmental influences on self-harm across adolescence and young adulthood (three time points). 

We fitted a trivariate Cholesky decomposition model to test stability and innovation in genetic and environmental influences on self-harm from ≤16 to 26. This model specified self-harm as a liability threshold variable. 

The Cholesky decomposition partitions A, C, and E components into three sets of factors acting at time 1 (T1), time 2 (T2), and time 3 (T3): AT1, CT1, and ET1 act on all three variables, AT2, CT2, and ET2 act on the second (age 21) and third (age 26) variables and AT3, CT3, and ET3 act only on the third (age 26) variable. 

Missing data were handled using full-information maximum likelihood estimates.
